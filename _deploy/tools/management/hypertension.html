1 Foundation

Set the blood-pressure target for this patient

Diagnosis is already confirmed (ABPM/HBPM ≥135/85, or clinic ≥140/90 with end-organ damage). Before anything else, agree the number you are treating to — it drives every step-up decision below.

Patient groupClinic targetABPM / HBPM
Age <80 years<140/90<135/85
Age ≥80 years<150/90<145/85
Type 2 diabetes (no CKD)<140/90 (<80y — NG136 unified in 2019)<135/85
CKD + diabetes, or CKD + ACR ≥70<130/80 (NG203)systolic 120–129
Postural drop / frailty / multimorbidityCheck lying→standing BP if ≥80y, T2DM or postural symptoms — significant drop = systolic ≥20 or diastolic ≥10 mmHg; then aim for the target on the standing BP. Clinical judgement over a strict number
A target makes the plan testable: at each review you either are or are not there, which removes ambiguity about whether to step up. Tighter targets in diabetes/CKD reflect their higher absolute cardiovascular and renal risk. In the very elderly or frail, over-treatment causes falls — the standing BP and the patient's function matter more than the headline figure.
2 Non-drug · every patient

Lifestyle prescription — a real treatment, not a delay tactic

Offer to every patient at every stage — before, alongside and after drugs. Each measure has a mechanism, a measurable target and a quantified BP effect. Agree one specific change today rather than reciting five.

🧂 Salt <6 g/day↓ plasma volume → ↓ cardiac output. Works in everyone (renin-independent). Avoid processed food; caution K⁺ salt-substitutes if on ACEi/ARB or CKD.<6 g/day↓4–5 mmHg
🥗 DASH diet↑ K⁺/Mg²⁺/Ca²⁺ → vasodilation + natriuresis. Veg, fruit, whole grains, low-fat dairy; ↓ red meat & sugar.5+ portions/day↓8–14 mmHg
🏃 Exercise↓ peripheral resistance via endothelial NO; ↓ sympathetic tone. Even 10-min daily walks help — start where they are.150 min/wk↓4–9 mmHg
⚖️ Weight↓ insulin resistance → ↓ RAAS. Frame: "losing 5 kg is like one BP tablet."BMI <25~1 mmHg / kg
🍺 AlcoholChronic intake → ↑ cortisol/RAAS; binges spike BP. AUDIT-C brief intervention.≤14 u/wk↓2–4 mmHg
🚭 SmokingDoesn't lower BP directly but is the biggest modifiable CV risk. Refer stop-smoking + NRT/varenicline.Cessation↓CVD 50%/yr
☕ CaffeineLimit excess (>4 strong coffees/day) — modest transient pressor effect.Moderate
😴 Sleep / OSAScreen for obstructive sleep apnoea if snoring + daytime somnolence — a treatable secondary contributor.STOP-BANG
Lifestyle changes alone can drop your blood pressure by as much as a tablet would. Let's pick just one to start with — which feels most doable for you?
Lifestyle is treatment, not a holding pattern: the DASH diet alone lowers systolic BP 8–14 mmHg — equivalent to a first-line drug. One specific, achievable, measurable target massively outperforms a generic list the patient will ignore. It continues after drugs start because it reduces the number of tablets ultimately needed.

3 Medication · stepwise

The A → C → D drug ladder

Start at Step 1 and step up only when BP stays above target. Before every step-up: confirm adherence, re-check HBPM, and reinforce lifestyle — do not add a drug to compensate for one that isn't being taken. Recheck U&Es 1–2 weeks after any ACEi/ARB or spironolactone change.

Step 1 Monotherapy — choice depends on profile titrate to max tolerated
<55y · not African / Afro-Caribbean
ACEi A
Ramipril 1.25–2.5 → 10 mg OD (or perindopril erbumine 4 → 8 mg, or lisinopril 10 → 20 mg). Switch to ARB — candesartan 8 → 32 mg (more effective & safer than losartan) — if dry cough.
≥55y · OR African / Afro-Caribbean (no DM)
CCB C
Amlodipine 5 → 10 mg OD. Troublesome ankle oedema → switch to lercanidipine 10 mg or felodipine. (Black + DM → ARB first.)
Type 2 diabetes · or CKD with ACR >3 (any age)
ACEi / ARB A
Renoprotective regardless of age/ethnicity. ARB first-line in Black patients.
BP ≥ target after 4 wk → check adherence, then ↓
Step 2 Dual therapy — A + C add the class not yet used
Add whichever of A or C is not already on board.
  • Combining two classes lowers BP more than doubling the dose of one.
  • ACEi + CCB has the strongest outcome evidence (ACCOMPLISH).
  • Consider a fixed-dose combination tablet (e.g. perindopril + amlodipine) to aid adherence.
BP ≥ target after 4 wk → check adherence, then ↓
Step 3 Triple therapy — A + C + D thiazide-like diuretic
Add D — indapamide 1.5 mg MR OD (preferred over bendroflumethiazide; better CV outcomes).
  • Check U&Es + eGFR before starting and at 4–6 weeks — can drop K⁺/Na⁺ (stop if Na⁺ <130) and precipitate gout. Bendroflumethiazide 2.5 mg is an alternative.
  • If already on a thiazide for another reason, that counts as D — don't double up diuretics.
Still ≥ target on optimal A+C+D = resistant HTN → ↓
Step 4 Resistant hypertension — add a 4th agent confirm true resistance first
First confirm it is real: adherence (consider urine drug screen), exclude white-coat (ABPM), exclude secondary cause. Then choose by serum potassium:
K⁺ ≤ 4.5 mmol/L
Spironolactone 25 mg OD
Most effective 4th agent (PATHWAY-2). Recheck U&Es/K⁺ at 1 month — esp. with ACEi/ARB.
K⁺ > 4.5 mmol/L
α-blocker (doxazosin) or β-blocker (bisoprolol)
Doxazosin IR 2–4 mg OD, or bisoprolol 2.5 mg OD (β-blocker if other indication — AF, IHD, HF). Doxazosin: ask about PDE5 inhibitors first.
⚠ Hyperkalaemia: ACEi/ARB + spironolactone needs close K⁺ monitoring — K⁺ >5.5 = stop + recheck. Still uncontrolled on 4 drugs → refer (see step 7).
The age/ethnicity split reflects renin physiology: younger non-Black patients have high-renin HTN that responds to RAAS blockade (A); older and Black patients have low-renin, volume-dependent HTN best controlled by a CCB or diuretic. β-blockers are not first-line for uncomplicated HTN — they re-enter only at Step 4 or when separately indicated. Spironolactone is the highest-yield 4th agent because resistant HTN is frequently aldosterone-driven.
4 Monitoring

Bloods after each change — the "1-2-1" rule

ACEi / ARB / spironolactone → U&Es within 1 week. Thiazide-like → within 2 weeks. Spironolactone at Step 4 → recheck at 1 month. Everyone → structured review once a year.

DrugTestWhenAction threshold
ACEi / ARBU&E + K⁺ + eGFR1 weekK⁺ rise >0.5 or creatinine ↑ >30% → stop + seek advice
Thiazide-likeNa⁺ + K⁺ + eGFR2 weeksK⁺ <3.5 → supplement/switch · Na⁺ <130 → stop urgently
SpironolactoneK⁺ + eGFR + Na⁺1 monthK⁺ >5.5 → stop + recheck in 1 week
All antihypertensivesBP · U&E · ACR · QRISKAnnuallyAbove target → titrate or add the next step
Most adverse events from a new antihypertensive happen in the first 1–4 weeks — a rising creatinine on an ACEi can signal undiagnosed renal artery stenosis, and hyperkalaemia is silent until dangerous. The blood test isn't a formality; it's how you catch harm before the patient feels it.

🔁 Not responding? Work through this before adding the next drug

"Apparent" resistance is usually adherence, technique or white-coat — not true pharmacological failure. Exclude each before escalating.

1 · AdherenceOpen, non-judgemental enquiry. Check pharmacy refill / prescription record. Consider once-daily / combination tablets.
2 · MeasurementCorrect cuff size, supported arm, both arms. Confirm with ABPM/HBPM to exclude white-coat effect.
3 · BP-raising drugsNSAIDs, oral steroids, COCP, venlafaxine, decongestants, liquorice, recreational stimulants, alcohol.
4 · Lifestyle driftSalt creep, weight gain, alcohol, reduced activity. Re-set one measurable target.
5 · Secondary causeYoung, abrupt, or truly resistant? Screen for Conn's, renal disease, phaeo, OSA (see refer).
6 · Optimise dosesAre existing drugs at maximum tolerated dose before a new one is added?
All excluded and adherent → return to the ladder and step up to the next rung
6 Safety-net

Safety-netting — say these out loud, document them

🔴 999 — all patients"Sudden severe headache unlike any before, weakness/numbness on one side, sudden vision change, chest pain, or confusion — call 999 immediately. Don't drive yourself; don't wait to ring us."
💊 Starting any drug"You may feel dizzy when standing, especially in week one — move slowly. If you feel faint, call us — but don't stop the tablet without speaking to us first."
🟠 On an ACEi"A dry tickly cough isn't dangerous — don't stop, ring us and we'll switch. But swelling of lips/tongue/throat is different — call 999."
⚖️ On a diuretic / spiro"If you get a vomiting-and-diarrhoea illness, pause these for 48h ('sick-day rules') and call us — they can affect your kidneys when you're dehydrated."
Naming the exact symptom and the exact action ("call 999", not "seek help") produces faster patient-activated escalation and is medico-legally protective — documented specific advice is a defence if the patient later deteriorates. Pre-warning about the ACEi cough prevents needless off-class switching; angioedema counselling is potentially life-saving.

✚ Confirm · don't miss

Masked hypertension & the ≥180/120 rules

SituationWhat it means / do
Masked hypertensionNormal clinic but high ABPM/HBPM — same risk as sustained HTN. Suspect in high-CV-risk patients with end-organ damage; don't be reassured by a good clinic reading.
≥180/120 + signsRetinal haemorrhage/papilloedema or chest pain/HF/AKI/neuro → same-day admission (accelerated hypertension).
≥180/120, no signsUrgent bloods + ACR + ECG, start/intensify treatment, repeat within 7 days — not 999, not routine.
Pregnancy ≥20wk, ≥140/90Same-day obstetric assessment (pre-eclampsia).

7 Refer to secondary care

When to step beyond primary care

Manage everything above in primary care first. Refer when you hit a ceiling or a red flag — keep treating in the interim unless instructed otherwise, and tell the patient the reason and what to expect.

Same-day / emergencyAccelerated HTN (papilloedema / retinal haemorrhage); BP ≥180/120 with new chest pain, breathlessness, neuro signs or AKI; suspected phaeo crisis; pregnancy ≥20 wk with BP ≥140/90.
Resistant HTNAbove target on optimal A + C + D + 4th agent, once adherence and white-coat are excluded → specialist hypertension clinic.
Suspected secondary causeHypokalaemia + HTN (Conn's); abdominal bruit / rapid eGFR fall (renal artery stenosis); paroxysmal sweating/palpitations (phaeo); resistant OSA.
Young patientAge <40 with confirmed hypertension — secondary screen and specialist assessment.
Specialist input neededIntolerance to multiple classes; eGFR <30 needing RAAS drugs; pregnancy / pre-conception planning; HTN with significant end-organ disease.
