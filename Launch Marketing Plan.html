A 52-year-old asks you for HRT. Flushes, broken sleep, low mood. Straightforward script? This is exactly where reasoning beats a quick prescription 👇 → Safety first. Any postmenopausal bleeding? A PV bleed more than 12 months after her last period is a 2-week-wait for endometrial cancer — investigated BEFORE any HRT. Never put unscheduled bleeding down to "hormones." → Route matters. Transdermal oestrogen (patch/gel) skips first-pass metabolism — lower VTE and stroke risk than oral. First choice if there's migraine, a raised BMI, or VTE risk. → Protect the endometrium. Uterus present + systemic oestrogen = she needs a progestogen (or a Mirena, which also covers contraception — because HRT isn't one). → Under 40 with POI? HRT until the natural age of menopause (~51) for bone and cardiovascular protection. That's one pathway in Reasoning GP — NICE NG23-aligned, red flags and referral thresholds built in. Make it land

Posting tips that matter

1 Post the link in the first comment

On LinkedIn, links in the body can suppress reach. Put "link in comments" and drop gpreasoning.uk as your first comment.

2 First 90 minutes decide reach

Reply to every comment fast. Early engagement is what pushes a post into more feeds — so post when you can be present.

3 One idea per post

Resist listing everything. Each post should leave the reader with a single takeaway and one clear action.

4 WhatsApp: ask permission in groups

A quick "OK to share a resource here?" to admins keeps you welcome — and stops the message being deleted as spam.

5 Track the code

Because LAUNCH7 is unique to these posts, redemptions tell you exactly which channel is working. Watch it in your access-codes console.

6 Repost the winner

Whichever Day 1–7 post performs best, adapt and re-run it in week 3. Repetition builds recognition — don't fear reusing a winner.
