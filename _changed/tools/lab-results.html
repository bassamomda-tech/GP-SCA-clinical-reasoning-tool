Lab results actioning — next steps, per result

Paste from EMIS / SystmOne or fill the form. The tool fires per-result rules from UK guidance (NICE CKS & NGs, BSH, Renal Association, BSG, BTA, BMJ Best Practice, Medscape) and turns each abnormal value into an action: emergency, 2WW, urgent, treat, drug review, repeat, refer, recall, or annotate — with the rule cited, a patient phrase, and a notes template you can copy.

1 · Patient context first

Age, sex, pregnancy, current meds — thresholds shift with each (e.g. K⁺ 5.6 on ACEi is a drug review; without it, just a repeat).

2 · Paste or type

The paste box parses common formats (Hb 102, K+ 6.2, HbA1c 62…). Anything it can't parse stays blank; fill it manually. Abnormal values get colour-coded as you type.

3 · Read per-result actions

The headline is the highest-acuity action. Below it: every rule that fired, grouped by test. Expand each for the patient phrase and notes template; one-click copy.

4 · Open the full pathway

Every analyte links to its full structured pathway (e.g. Hb → Anaemia, TSH → Abnormal TFTs) — all 52 lab pathways are wired in. Rules also hand off to the Triage Tool and MedChooser where relevant.

Educational use only. Reference ranges, drug thresholds, and pathways vary by lab, assay, age, sex, pregnancy, and locality. The tool surfaces published UK guidance — always verify against live NICE CKS, your local pathology lab ranges, your formulary, and your clinical judgement. Recommendations are illustrative, not clinical advice.
