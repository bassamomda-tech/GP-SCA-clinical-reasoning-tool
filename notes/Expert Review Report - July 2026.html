1.The verdict at a glance

This is the broadest single GP education + point-of-care product I have seen in the UK market — nothing else combines reasoning pathways, management protocols, a 312-case SCA suite with AI roleplay, a grounded clinical Q&A assistant, an ambient scribe and automatic CPD in one free product. The clinical writing is examiner-grade in most places sampled. What separates it from being genuinely competitive is not features — it is trust signalling and update governance: a handful of real currency errors exist (ranitidine, pre-2023 FIT guidance), and no page tells the reader when it was last reviewed or by whom. Competitors win users on exactly that axis.

2.Strengths no other UK platform provides

① Reasoning-first pathways with "Why?" drawers — a category of one

Every pathway sampled pairs the what with an evidence-literate why: likelihood ratios (chest-wall tenderness LR− 0.3), NNTs, mortality asymmetries, medico-legal framing. Chest Pain alone carries ~9 reasoning essays behind its steps. NICE CKS tells you the rule; GPnotebook gives you the fact; nobody else teaches the reasoning at the point of the rule.

vs market: CKS/GPnotebook are reference-flat; BMJ Best Practice has evidence but is hospital-flavoured and paywalled; Red Whale teaches reasoning but only in courses/handbooks, not as interactive pathways.

② NG12 cancer-pathway discipline baked into every relevant page

The purple 2WW pill with precise NG12 wording (age thresholds, direct-access tests, FIT/CA-125/PSA values) appears in both the Safety and Refer nodes of pathway after pathway. The Scribe even flags 2WW criteria it detects in a transcript, and the live test proved it: the haematuria example generated a note correctly invoking the ≥45 visible-haematuria urology 2WW. This is the single most examiner-pleasing and patient-safety-relevant design decision on the site.

vs market: no SCA platform and no scribe I'm aware of systematically cross-checks NG12; iatroX cites it reactively when asked.

③ The SCA Hot Seat's case depth beats the market leader per-case

Sampled cases (obstetric cholestasis, febrile 7-week-old) each carry: a hidden agenda with psychology, full ICE, ≥8 behavioural checkpoints, a phrase-by-phrase worked consultation, a guideline knowledge panel with an explicit "Never do" list, and correct NICE anchoring (NG143 red flag correct). Plus a 12-minute timer, audio/video recording, AI patient roleplay with voice, and transcripts — all free.

vs market: SCAreVision (£11.99–15.99/mo) has ~350–375 cases + videos + group mode; SCA Prep and MedTutor sell AI roleplay alone. Your per-case teaching depth (worked phrasing + never-do lists + hidden-agenda psychology) is richer than the samples those platforms show publicly.

④ Marking that matches the real exam's arithmetic

The "Real SCA domains" tool grades 0–3 per domain with Clinical Management & Medical Complexity weighted ×1.5 → 10.5 per case → 126 per diet. That is the actual SCA scoring skeleton, and almost no free resource reproduces it. The domain-trend log ("anything averaging below 2.0 is the domain to train") is exactly how a trainer reads diet feedback.

⑤ Ask: a grounded assistant with an honesty architecture

Live-tested with a deliberately colloquial stem ("70yo man can't wee, dribbling… waterworks"): the answer led with the right safety move (exclude acute retention), then PVR, then BPH vs cancer with 2WW awareness. Under the hood: hybrid lexical+semantic retrieval over the site's own 975 topics, a colloquial→clinical synonym map, web search restricted to trusted UK domains, a vetted answer cache with owner approval, a 45-question gold test bank, and thumbs feedback. That is a more serious quality flywheel than most funded products ship.

vs market: iatroX (free, MHRA Class I, citation-first) and Heidi Evidence are the benchmark; your differentiator is that answers point back into your own pathways and tools, turning an answer into a next action.

⑥ The flywheel between tools — the real moat

Scribe consultations can be marked like an SCA station; every pathway read accrues auto-CPD; audios log listening time; Ask deep-links to the pathway it answered from; the local-area picker adapts prescribing advice to the user's ICB. Each tool feeds another. Competitors are point solutions; this is a system.

⑦ Guideline currency where it matters most — asthma

The chronic asthma protocol is fully on the joint NICE/BTS/SIGN NG245 (2024) pathway: no SABA-only, AIR → MART ladder with named licensed devices, FeNO + eosinophils before escalation, the 6-puff acute rule. Plenty of paid resources and many practices are still teaching the 2019 ladder. This is a marketing-grade proof point.

Errors found fix these

Error Ranitidine recommended — withdrawn from the UK market (2020, NDMA contamination)

tools/algorithms/chest-pain.html (GORD branch: "Ranitidine 300 mg nocte if PPI contraindicated") · tools/algorithms/nvp.html (Step 4: "ranitidine 150mg BD")

A trainee following this cannot prescribe it; an examiner would mark it as outdated knowledge. It has not returned to UK supply.

Fix: replace with famotidine (the H2 antagonist now used when a PPI is unsuitable — also the RCOG/UKTIS position for pregnancy in the NVP pathway).

Error Chest Pain contradicts your own (correct) Dysphagia pathway on the NG12 rule

tools/algorithms/chest-pain.html — Gastroenterology referral row: "Dysphagia (2WW if age >55 + weight loss)"

NG12 mandates urgent direct-access OGD for unexplained dysphagia at any age, alone — which dysphagia.html states perfectly ("no such thing as benign new dysphagia"). The chest-pain page waters it down to an age+weight-loss condition. Internal inconsistency on a cancer criterion is the most damaging kind of error for credibility.

Fix: change the row to "Dysphagia → 2WW upper-GI endoscopy at any age (NICE NG12)". Worth grepping all 270 for other pages that mention dysphagia in passing.

Outdated stance Rectal bleeding still teaches the pre-2023 "don't wait for FIT" rule

tools/algorithms/rectal-bleeding.html (Refer node + Investigations node, two places)

Since the Aug 2023 NG12/DG56 update, FIT (≥10 µg/g) guides referral for nearly all suspected-colorectal-cancer presentations — including rectal bleeding — with referral without FIT reserved for rectal/anal mass or anal ulceration, and FIT-negative patients not routinely referred (safety-netted instead). Your page's age thresholds are right, but "do not wait for FIT if NG12 criteria met" is the old doctrine. Notably, competitor SCA Prep's public demo answers this exact question FIT-first — a candidate using both would see the conflict.

Fix: reframe both nodes FIT-first per the May 2025 NG12 PDF in uploads/; keep the "mass/ulceration → refer without FIT" exceptions and the "visible haemorrhoids don't exclude cancer" caution (which is excellent).

Citation currency Acute asthma cites NG80/BTS-SIGN — superseded by NG245

tools/algorithms/acute-asthma.html footer ("BTS/SIGN 2023, NICE NG80 2017 updated 2024")

The acute treatment content itself is correct and safe. But NG80 and the separate BTS/SIGN guideline were replaced by the joint NG245 (Nov 2024) — which your own chronic-asthma protocol already uses. An AKT-sitting trainee will notice the mismatch. Also consider a line on acute advice for patients on AIR/MART (the chronic page has it; the acute page assumes SABA).

Fix: update the footer citation, and cross-link "on MART? see the NG245 acute-dosing note" between the two pages.

Naming drift "IAPT" used in ~6 pathways — renamed NHS Talking Therapies (2023)

chest-pain, constipation, tinnitus, dysphagia (globus row) and others; low-mood and sciatica already use the correct "NHS Talking Therapies (formerly IAPT)" formulation

Fix: site-wide find-and-replace to the low-mood page's formulation. Cheap win for currency perception.

Precision Ask answer gave a flat "PSA >4 ng/mL" rather than age-specific thresholds

pages/ask.js FRAMING (the model, not the library — your high-psa pathway is correctly age-specific)

Fix: add one line to the Ask framing: "PSA is interpreted against age-specific thresholds (never quote a single cut-off)" — and consider adding a PSA item to the gold test bank so regressions get caught.

4.Weaknesses to fix — prioritised

P0 — trust & safety (do before promoting the site)

  • No "last reviewed" date, reviewer, or version on any clinical page. This is the #1 gap vs every credible competitor: SCAreVision leads with "written by examiners, checked against current guidelines, updated as guidance changes"; iatroX leads with MHRA registration. A clinician deciding whether to trust a pathway mid-consult looks for a date first. Add a review stamp line to the alg-foot of every pathway (one shared-file change can render it from a per-page data attribute).
  • Fix the errors in §3 — ranitidine ×2, the dysphagia contradiction, FIT-first. Small edits, outsized credibility cost if a user finds them first.
  • The free-model fallback answers clinical questions. When Anthropic credit runs out, Ask silently degrades to a general-purpose free model with only a small ⚠ note. For a tool explicitly positioned for point-of-care use, consider making the fallback banner unmissable (colour-block the whole answer) or restricting fallback answers to library-grounded extracts only.
  • Vetted answer cache has no guideline-change expiry. An owner-approved cached answer persists indefinitely; if NICE updates, the cache serves the old answer instantly and confidently. Add a "purge/flag cache on library update" rule or a visible cached-on date.

P1 — competitive gaps (what paying alternatives have that you don't)

  • No consultation videos. SCAreVision's premium tier is essentially 70 videos of pass vs fail with examiner commentary — the single most-cited reason trainees pay. Your "Worked Examples" text tool is the nearest equivalent; even audio-acted versions of 10 of your best cases (you already have an audio pipeline) would close much of this.
  • No group-practice mode. RCGP guidance says don't revise the SCA alone, and SCAreVision's QR three-role mode (candidate/patient/marker) directly serves that. Your cases actually print/read well as role-play scripts — a lightweight "share this case: patient view / marker view" link would be a cheap, differentiated answer.
  • Hot Seat scorecard domains don't use the exam's names. Tasks / Relating to Others / Global Skills trains good behaviours, but the exam marks Data Gathering & Diagnosis / Clinical Management & Medical Complexity / Relating to Others. The bridge exists (the Real Domains tool) but an ST3 comparing you to SCAreVision — which marks in exam language everywhere — may perceive misalignment. Consider renaming or dual-labelling the Hot Seat scorecard.
  • No AKT support. Every SCA-platform comparison notes candidates also need AKT prep; you have 435 articles and a qbank engine (sca-qbank) already — an AKT-style MCQ mode over the article library is adjacent, not new, work.
  • Scribe has no EHR integration or clinical-safety file (DCB0129/DTAC), so it can't compete with Heidi/Accurx for real clinical use — which is fine and correctly disclaimed, but the positioning should lean harder into what it uniquely does: the only scribe that marks your consultation like an SCA examiner. That's a trainee product nobody else has.

P2 — experience & polish

  • ST1 overwhelm / no guided journey. The nav offers Ask, Compass, Prescribing, Compendium, More, SCA mode, 14 SCA tools, hubs… An ST1 doesn't know whether to start with pathways, cases or audios. A "New to GP? Your first month" rail (6 tiles, one per week-task) would convert the site's breadth from intimidating to structured.
  • Header crowding at tablet widths (~900–950px): Sign in / Sign up labels wrap mid-word, and on the Ask page the Chats / New chat bar crowds the nav row. Cosmetic but visible on every page.
  • Pathway length vs the 10-minute claim. Chest Pain is ~9 dense steps and several thousand words; brilliant for study, heavy mid-consult. A per-pathway "60-second version" (the diagram view partially serves this) or a collapsed-by-default mode for the kv tables would serve the in-clinic user the site targets with Ask.
  • Search/entry from symptoms could be more forgiving — Ask handles colloquial language well ("waterworks" resolved correctly in testing), but the global search is lexical; consider routing zero-hit searches into Ask automatically.
  • Preview/UI regression risk: during this session the Hot Seat page could not be visually captured (environment issue rather than a site fault — no console errors; earlier sessions verified it). Worth a quick manual open of tools/sca-practice.html on the live site after the next upload, since it's the heaviest page (80 case files).
