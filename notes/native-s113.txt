Mr. Brown, from everything you've told me about your symptoms—feeling breathless
when you exert yourself, but improving with rest—I am concerned that this could be
a condition called angina. I’d like to explain what that means and what we can do
about it. Angina happens when the heart muscle doesn’t get enough oxygen-rich
blood, usually because of narrowing in the blood vessels that supply the heart.
This can cause symptoms like chest tightness, discomfort, or even breathlessness,
especially during activities like walking or climbing stairs.
Management
• Offer face to face to see and assess- listen to heart sounds, listen to chest,
check BP, etc
• Offer ECG, bloods for BNP, FBC, Cholesterol, Chest Xray
• Offer GTN spray and Aspirin 75mg OD- heavily safety net- To use GTN spray
during an episode of SOB, take a second dose after 5 minutes if the pain has
not eased, Call 999 for an ambulance if the pain has not eased 5 minutes
after the second dose, or earlier if the pain is intensifying or the person is
unwell
• Offer referral to Rapid access chest pain clinic
• Safety net re unstable angina – if worsening SOB at rest, chest pain,
palpitations, dial 999
Lesson from this case: Shortness of breath (SOB) can be the only symptom of
angina, often misattributed to COPD. Key clues for angina include exertional
onset, intermittent, relief with rest/GTN, and absence of cough/wheeze. COPD-
related SOB is persistent, relieved by inhalers, and often accompanied by
cough/sputum. Always assess for underlying cardiac causes!
