Marking Scheme
Data Gathering and Diagnosis
• Ask about the shortness of breath — when he noticed it getting worse and
whether the onset was gradual or sudden (gradual worsening may indicate
COPD progression, while sudden onset could suggest an infective
exacerbation or cardiac cause)
• Ask what makes the breathlessness worse and what helps relieve it
• Ask how far patient can walk before getting breathless (e.g. MRC Dyspnoea
Scale — stopping after about 100 metres suggests MRC grade 4)
• Ask if patient experiences orthopnoea (breathlessness when lying flat),
paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnoea (waking at night gasping for air), or any leg
swelling, to screen for heart failure.
• Ask about associated cough, and if present, whether it is productive and the
nature of the sputum
• Ask about haemoptysis
• Ask about associated wheeze, palpitations, or chest pain.
