1.What the sweep confirmed

The clinical content of the algorithm layer is sound — the red-flag scan found no new dangerous advice beyond the items fixed in earlier rounds. But the footer citations were systematically unreliable: roughly 1 in 7 guideline numbers was wrong, in a very recognisable way — the right guideline name, the wrong number (usually a NG/CG prefix swap or a number belonging to a different guideline entirely).

Why this matters: a trainee revising for the AKT memorises these numbers; a sceptical GP checks one citation, finds "NICE NG198 Gallstone Disease" is actually the acne guideline, and stops trusting the whole site. This class of error is now cleared from all three content layers (protocols ✓ round 2, algorithms ✓ this round, quick-fix items ✓ round 1).
