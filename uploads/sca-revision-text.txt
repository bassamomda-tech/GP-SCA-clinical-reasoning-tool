Cues not to miss:
• Mum anxious and worried
ICE:
• Ideas: Allergic to something
• Concern: more severe reaction can be life threatening
• Expectation: adrenaline autoinjector/ allergy tests/ advice what to do
Management vital points
• Explain allergen as something you’ve eaten, inhaled or come in contact and the
body immune system respond to it as harmful. Angioedema is a deeper form of
hives which occurs deep in the skin and more around lips and eyes. Risk of
anaphylaxis which involve breathing problems and drop in BP which require
immediate care
• If on ACEI – stop immediately
• Allergy diary to record any possible trigger + referral to allergy clinic to do some
allergy test if no identifiable trigger + avoidance of any possible triggers

• To discuss adrenaline autoinjectors with specialist via A&G if at risk of anaphylaxis
(coexisting asthma/COPD/heart disease/ can’t avoid allergy/ happen with trace
amount of allergen)
• Safety netting if anaphylaxis to call 999 + give adrenaline autoinjector and lie flat
with raised legs then use 2nd autoinjector if needed after 5 minutes if no better.
• Discontinue BBs if adrenaline autoinjector is needed
